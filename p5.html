Project #5 Optical Illusion

Description

Pattern-making is an ancient and culturally diverse practice. For this project, I decided to look back a popular pattern of the Brazilian culture. Then, to use reproduce the pattern in the digital form, while creating an optical illusion.

Design Process

When we (Brazilians) think about Copacabana, in Rio de Janeiro, this pattern comes to our minds almost instantly. Supposedly, it represents an harmonious encounter of the beach and the city. Harmonious... If we ignore the pollution that has been plaguing some areas. In spite of that, the pattern became a signature of the shore and it's design is made out of white calcite and black basalt from Portugal, our beloved Colonizers ((contain irony)). In fact, the pattern is very common between lusophone countries. Although most Brazilians think it is unique and exclusive from their country, the route is actually based on Praça do Rocio, in Lisbon.

So I decided to design a new interpretation of this iconic and dubious pattern. Thanks to Plural Artes video (see credits), I was able to break down the pattern. At first I thoght about translating it to full circles, but after soe failures, I realized that would need to work with half circles - aka arcs - to reach the combination I wanted.

Reflection

The most difficult part of the creation was to understand the process behind the pattern. How to retro-enginner it? What kind of primitive shapes we can encounter within it? What is the frequency each shape is repeated? How do I represent that in the code? In that sense, modules became my best friends. Once I figured out how to break that pattern in pieces, it became more natural to assemble it all again.
