Project #4 Exquisite Corpse

Description

Exquisite corpse is a surrealist technique to create surprising and unexpected forms of narratives. It's a collaboration between multiple people to sew a exquisite and oddly delightful figure.

Design Process

As the Exquisite Corpse process demands, we began by drawing our creatures/beings on our own. No interference or constraints. No references. We could design whatever we wanted. The result was the following:

  • The Dragon, from Beatriz (myself)
  • The Lizard, from Myles
  • Their name is Daniel and they are hopelessly in love with the idea of rainy days, but unfortunately they live in Arizona. Optimistic as ever, Daniel dons a rain jacket and boots every day in hopes of manifesting the perfect rainy day
  • The Robot, from Lynette
  • My robot is drew based on my favorite kitchen supplies, which have been my best friends since the quarantine time.

Each of us would keep our own head - which is good, isn't it? tsc - and s wap our corpse's body and bottom with one another. I ended up with Myles' body and Lynette's, while each of them got the same part of my corpse.

    Later, starting with our own remained part (head) we would re-create our own interpretation of that exquisite being. In order to better visualize this, I went to editing software and made a clumsy collage. The challenge I've found here was to understand who could I reinterpreted those detail and way different styles within my own.

    Reflection

    While creating this being, I've noticed many dissonances between the original pieces and final one. From color palette to angle to contour to each style itself. The difficulty I've found was not in coding, but in translating those details without losing the personality I was seeing in the originals or even without interfering on my own style.

    So it's interesting to me how difficult it is to translate and unify parts in order to create a whole. Simplification or complexization of figures is a field to be addressed with more time and through practice. Abstracting something that it's not clear in your mind is a damanding challenge. So is the understading of your own style and visual signature - or lacking of it.
