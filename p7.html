Project 7# Autobiographical Game

Description

Every one of us has a story, a narrative that we either tell to ourselves or others, about who we are. This narrative tells a story of a ghost who lives between two worlds. Everything the ghost does reminds it of the burden of not fitting the binary. Ghost loves humans, other ghosts and other creatures who live in between too. But not everyone understands. Ghost speaks human and ghost language. So sometimes is stressful to deal with a messy brain it is. Ghost likes numbers and exact things. But ghost also likes to talk about things of beings. Ghost is multi. Multi ethnic, multi cultured, multi liguistic. Ghost sometimes is so "in between" that it feels unseen... Outcasted. Could Ghost be considered a shapeshipfter? Or just someone who's is all, not one? One who is learning how to live between two worlds.

Design Process

I'm biracial, bisexual, bilingual. My ancetors came from two different continents. I grew up in a world of "twos." Poor neighborhood, but had access to many different opportunities. Catholic, then Buddhist, then both, then none. I love the exact sciences. But I also love to study human condition. All that (and more) took me to a simple mechanic of "shapeshifting". Changing colors. Facing the world through different/multiple lenses. Which makes me change... Constantly...

Reflection

Building this game revealed to me the pros and cons of abstraction. I had to abstract facts of my own live into mechanics. That way I could have a system simple enough gamify and program. However, this made me discard several details, to the point that the essence of my story was no longer clear - at least not as I wish it was.
