Project #3 Experimental Clock

Description

Conventional clocks mark time according to a standardized 24 hour cycle. This approach, however, is not universal in terms of capturing and measuring of experience. So this project was developed to show time in a way that orginary clocks fail. The clocks you see here are personal and subjective. They are directly related to my own perception of time.

Design Process

I'd started my process by reading and reflecting about our relation with time. I checked dictionaries, myths and I thought about our mannerisms while talking about time. So my first idea came out of how we report time to others. If it's 8:37:46, we will often say 8:30 (eight thirty). In that sense, I would illustrate time as fading. Minutes and seconds would stay behind hours. Different opacities for different weights.

But I came up with a few questions that I wanted to ruminate on... Why do we think time goes by differently at certain occasions? Why/When do say that we hate it? Why/When do we love it? Why/When we don't want to forget it? That sparked a memory of a curious object that I saw in books and movies from the Harry Potter saga.

"A Remembrall is a magical large marble-sized glass ball that contains smoke which turns red when its owner or user has forgotten something. It turns clear once whatever was forgotten is remembered."

The idea here would be to convert that object into a time object. You would shake a glass ball to make the time appear and then shake again to make it dissapear. Cause sometimes we just want to forget about time anyway...

Then I had my last and favorite idea... Starting from the idea of how I perceived time differently depending on what I am doing... what I am thinking. Each clock has a different disturbance. A variable that I've created as a way to translate/mesasure how much certain events in my life annoy me/make me anxious.

One of the clocks - the one in the middle, would be just like any other clock. It would perceive time like I wish that I perceived time. Hours, minutes and seconds. Then, by moving the mouse, I would had many other clocks bringing up questions related to my life and its conundrums...

"Did you finish your assignment?","Wheres is your visa?" Those are the clocks that disturb me the most. One is fast and wobbly. So many things to do... So little time... The other clock goes backwards. Also, shaky. After being in a immigration process for so long, sometimes it feels like it's never going to happen. Like it gets more distant every day.

Finally, my last two clocks. "When are you going to visit your grandma?" and "When will you be ok?" Both questions used to trouble me a lot a while ago. But I notice how I don't get as much disturbed like used to. I've learned that missing my family is part of the process of building a new life somewhere. I've that I AM OK. I've been ok since the beginning and I'll continue to be.

Reflection

Time is a curse and a medicine. It makes us look at problems through a different lense, depending on the occasion. It tranforms one in pure anxiety and it brings confort to others. It's equal and it's not. It's a exact system but it's a system inserted within a social one, which is not exact at all.
