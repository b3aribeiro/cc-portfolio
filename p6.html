Project #6 Data Portrait

Description

Data can be scary. It can point to numerous flaws and mistakes of humanity. But data is humane! So aside from graphics and probabilities, data can be beautiful. It can teach something about The Other or about yourself. Starting from that point, I decided to humanize my data by keeping track of multiple habits for an entire week. Those were routine habits associated with my feelings - read stress and joy - in one way or another. For instance, I was tracking the amount of entertainment I consume daily. That's because when I'm happy and relaxed, I tend to watch more movies and play video games. When I'm sad or anxious, I only listen to music or watch something shorter. After collecting enough data, I translated the possible outcomes in a visual representation to create a data-self portrait for each day.

My data portrait is assembled by the following outputs...

  • 1. My overall sentiment for that day.
  • 2. If my husband had somehow annoyed me or not.
  • 3. My water intake - since I tend to drink less water (and take less care of myself) when I'm in stressed or busy.
  • 4. The people - from my homecountry - that I talked to, if any.
  • 5. If I consumed any form of entretainment that day.
  • 6. The places I'd been that day.
  • 7. Last but not least, how many times I'd regreted doing something that day - could be eating too many sweets, shopping unnecessary stuff or itching too much.

    Reflection

    The process of self-tracking gave me not only insights about myself but about data as a whole. I was able to realize how anxious I am yet, how hard - meaning close to impossible - is to track and expose my anxiety behavior through meaningful and precise data.

    Through this project, I was also able to realize how data is a very delicate object to work with. For instance, I was keeping track of my water intake. I decided to do that specifically because, for the past few weeks, I was drinking very little water. About 1 liter a day - which is terrible. However, once I decided to track my intake, I started to drink more water! In the end, to some extend, I felt like I was biasing/corrupting my data. For an external eye, it will look like I drink water regularly, but in the long run, I know that don't. The data was just forcing me to behave differently.
